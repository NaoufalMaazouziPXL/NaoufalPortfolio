workplace learning

Follow my journey through wpl 1

I participated in Workplace Learning 1 (WPL 1), which focused on self-reflection, branding assignments, designing a dashboard, and creating our portfolio using HTML and CSS. Throughout WPL 1, we explored various aspects of digital design, including creating a cohesive brand identity, developing interactive dashboards, and showcasing our skills through portfolio websites. The main objective of WPL 1 was to provide hands-on experience and enhance our understanding of digital design principles. We delved into self-reflection exercises to better understand our strengths and areas for improvement. Additionally, we worked on branding assignments, where we learned how to create visually appealing and cohesive brand elements.

Another significant aspect of WPL 1 was designing a dashboard. We gained insights into user experience (UX) design and focused on creating intuitive interfaces that prioritize usability. This involved wireframing, prototyping, and implementing the designs using HTML and CSS. Furthermore, we utilized our HTML and CSS skills to develop our portfolio websites. This allowed us to showcase our projects, skills, and experiences in a visually appealing manner. By creating our portfolios, we learned how to effectively communicate our design abilities and leave a lasting impression on potential employers or clients. Overall, WPL 1 provided a comprehensive learning experience in digital design and branding. Through self-reflection, branding assignments, dashboard design, and portfolio development, we acquired practical skills and a deeper understanding of the design process. This foundation sets the stage for future challenges and opportunities in the field of digital design.

Follow my journey through wpl 2

I worked on Workplace Learning 2 (WPL 2), which aims to create an environment that replicates real-world experiences for digital design. In WPL 2, participants are assigned to teams and tasked with creating a dummy company. The project involves various design and development activities, including designing and developing the website, designing the app, creating the brand book, designing the logo, and conducting interviews.

The goal of WPL 2 is to provide a hands-on learning experience where participants can apply their digital design skills in a practical setting. By working on these diverse tasks, participants gain valuable experience in creating a cohesive brand identity, developing digital assets, and engaging in interview processes. This immersive approach helps participants develop a well-rounded skill set and prepares them for real-world design challenges in the field of digital design and branding.
